## Assessment

### Researcher Workload: MANAGEABLE but OVERPRODUCING

The researcher is not overloaded in terms of coverage — 14 topics across 2 vendors is well within a single sweep session's capacity. Sweep quality is consistently high. The issue is **output volume, not workload**: 8 proposals in 2 days, 33 total proposal files, growing ADOPT backlog.

**Diagnosis**: The researcher treats every analysis gap as a proposal-worthy item. Many proposals are detailed implementation specs for work weeks away (OTEL tracing, session rewind, four-topology dispatch). These proposals consume researcher time to write, factory-steward time to read, and discussion time to debate — then get DEFER'd anyway.

**Recommendation**: Direct the researcher to use a **proposal threshold**: only write a standalone proposal file if the item requires implementation details beyond what the ADOPT verdict captures. Discussion verdicts ("ADOPT P1: add OTEL to Phase 5 requirements") are often sufficient for factory-steward action without a separate 40-line proposal.

### Factory-Steward Bottleneck: MILD

The factory-steward processed ADOPT items from Apr 17 proposals in its Apr 18 session (commit `3bee032`). The ADOPT-to-implementation conversion rate appears healthy for P0/P1 items. The backlog is growing for P2 items, but P2 items are explicitly lower priority.

**No bottleneck on critical-path work.** The bottleneck is on speculative/future items (Phase 5 design notes, MCP security elevation) that can reasonably wait.

### Pipeline Cadence: APPROPRIATE

Two cycles per day (night: 2-4 AM, morning: 10 AM-12 PM) provides sufficient coverage for a period of rapid change (Opus 4.7 launch, Haiku 3 retirement, pre-I/O monitoring). When the ecosystem stabilizes (post-I/O, late May), consider reducing to once daily.

### Team Composition: NO CHANGES NEEDED

Previous considerations for splitting the researcher (Anthropic-specialist + Google-specialist) are not justified:
- Google surfaces are in deep freeze — a dedicated Google agent would have nothing to report most days.
- The single researcher handles the cross-pollination analysis well (finding ADK patterns applicable to our Anthropic-based pipeline).
- Adding an agent adds cost and coordination overhead without clear benefit.

## Specific Recommendations

1. **Reduce proposal generation rate**: Cap at 4-5 proposals per discussion, max 2 at P0/P1. Quality filter: "Would this proposal tell factory-steward something the ADOPT verdict doesn't?"
2. **Compress frozen-topic reporting**: Topics stable for 3+ sweeps get one-line updates, not paragraphs.
3. **No team structure changes** for the next 2 weeks. Re-evaluate after Google I/O (May 19-20) when Google surfaces unfreeze and sweep workload may increase significantly.
4. **Consider reducing to 1 cycle/day post-I/O** if the daily volume of new findings drops below 3 actionable items per sweep.

## Cost Assessment

Current research pipeline cost (estimated from session durations in perf JSONs):
- Researcher: ~25-40 min/session × 2 sessions/day = 50-80 min/day
- Research-lead: ~15-20 min/session × 2 sessions/day = 30-40 min/day (new)
- Factory-steward ADOPT implementation: ~30-60 min/session × 2 sessions/day = 60-120 min/day

Total: ~140-240 min/day (~2.3-4.0 hours/day) for the research pipeline.

Adding the research-lead agent increases daily cost by ~30-40 min. This is justified if it:
- Reduces wasted researcher output (fewer proposals that get DEFER'd)
- Focuses factory-steward on higher-value ADOPT items
- Catches quality issues before they propagate

**Assessment**: Justified for now. Re-evaluate after 1 week of directive-guided research output.

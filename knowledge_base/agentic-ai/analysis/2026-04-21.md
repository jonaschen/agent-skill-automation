## Cross-Pollination Opportunities

### 1. A2A Agent Cards ↔ Fleet Manifest (S3)
A2A's Agent Card schema provides a standardized identity layer. Our fleet manifest should adopt it as the canonical format, adding our pipeline-specific extensions (status, cron schedule) as custom fields. This makes the manifest useful both internally (fleet management) and externally (A2A interop) with zero additional work.

### 2. Gemma 4 LiteRT-LM ↔ Phase 6 Runtime (Phase 6 de-risk)
LiteRT-LM's "4K tokens + 2 skills in <3s" benchmark maps directly to our SKILL.md execution model. If Phase 6 adopts LiteRT-LM instead of (or alongside) ONNX/GGUF, skill definitions could execute on-device without modification. Worth tracking as a runtime candidate when Phase 6 design begins.

### 3. Mariner Teach & Repeat ↔ S1 Self-Improvement (long-term)
Mariner's pattern of learning from human demonstrations is orthogonal to our optimize-eval loop. Our loop improves descriptions (how agents are triggered); Teach & Repeat improves behaviors (what agents do). A hybrid approach — optimize-eval for routing + demonstration learning for behavior — could be a Phase 7+ research direction.

### 4. ADK v2.0 Workflow(BaseNode) ↔ Phase 5 TCI Routing (S2)
ADK v2.0.0a3's graph-based orchestration (lazy scan dedup + partial resume) is architecturally similar to our planned Phase 5 TCI routing. When ADK v2.0 GA ships (I/O timeframe), a detailed comparison against our design should assess whether adopting ADK's primitives (on the Google track) is more efficient than building a parallel system. This is the highest-priority cross-pollination for S2.

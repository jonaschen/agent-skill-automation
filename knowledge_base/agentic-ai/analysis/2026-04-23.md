## 3. Architectural Threats & Risk Mitigation

### 3.1 Goal Hijacking (OWASP #1)
*   **Threat**: The OWASP Agentic Top 10 identifies "Goal Hijacking" as the primary risk for autonomous agents.
*   **Pipeline Risk**: As we move to Phase 5 (Multi-Agent), sub-agents might deviate from the lead's original objective during deep tool-calling chains.
*   **Mitigation**: Implement a "Goal-Consistency Hook" in `post-tool-use.sh`. Every 5 tool calls, the agent must perform a "Self-Correction Reflection" comparing current state to the initial Preamble goal.

### 3.2 Cost Amplification in "Deep Research Max"
*   **Threat**: Google's "Deep Research Max" success confirms that "test-time compute" (long-horizon thinking) is the new scaling law.
*   **Pipeline Risk**: Our current cost-ceiling logic is per-session. A "Deep Research" session could easily exhaust the daily budget before the ceiling triggers.
*   **Mitigation**: Implement "Incremental Budget Gates" (Phase 5.4 Watchdog). Halt and require human approval if a single task segment exceeds 25% of the total session budget.

### 3.3 A2A vs. MCP vs. Agent SDK
*   **Threat**: Fragmentation of agent standards.
*   **Strategic Stance**: 
    - **MCP** for Tool Discovery/Execution (Hardware/Data).
    - **A2A** for Identity and Governance (Security/Routing).
    - **Agent SDK** for Session Lifecycle (State/Persistence).
*   Avoid picking one; build the "transpiler" layer (Task 5.3.4) to bridge them.
